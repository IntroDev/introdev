Try Catch Software was founded by Tim Wardle, an experienced software developer, architect and technical leader. An overview of his journey and experience is outlined below.

Try Catch Software Managing Director

Tim set up Try Catch Software to help software companies become more effective by drawing on his years of experience managing development teams and designing and building software products.

Agile and lean principles are at the core of most of Tim's thinking. Through these ideas he aims to provide a bridge between a business and its customers' needs and the technologies and best practices involved in building great software.

April 2015

Click Innovation Technology Director

As Technology Director Tim guides the technical direction of the company and their CRM product, Lighthouse. Whilst a strategic role Tim believe that staying hands-on is the best way of keeping up to date and identifying areas for improvement. He regularly partakes in strategic meetings with colleagues and clients but can also be found fixing bugs, refactoring or designing user interfaces.

Tim continues to work with Click Innovation and acts as a non-executive Technology Director.

September 2014

Click Innovation Development Manager

After joining Click Innovation Tim managed a restructuring of the development department and built a team to develop a new SaaS CRM product called Lighthouse that was to be built on new technologies with development best practices.

Tim architected and built large parts of the Lighthouse core and delivered the product to market. He also introduced some bold changes in team behaviour such as test-driven development and pair programming that proved to be beneficial to productivity, quality and team morale. Tim aimed to create a culture of innovation and attention to detail.

April 2012

Protec Innovations Technical Architect

As part of a growing team with a maturing codebase Tim moved into an architectural role to oversee all technical aspects of the business. His responsibilities included guiding technology selection, software architecture, server administration, deployment automation and encouraging development best-practices.

January 2011

Protec Innovations Lead Developer

Tim was first developer on the Phase4 project, a piece of software to manage the after-sales service industry. Tim designed and built majority of the Phase4 codebase based on the business requirements provided by the in-house industry expert. His management role grew as more staff joined the team, requiring changes to the project management process. He managed the adoption of Scrum and later Kanban.

September 2007

University of Warwick MEng in Computer Science

Whilst at Warwick Tim learnt the computer science and software engineering principles that underpin everything he knows today. This deep understanding allows him to quickly learn, understand and assess new technologies and their suitability in solving real-world problems.

September 2003
